## Findings

1. The route now pins NexusEngine commit `38229f59c22cb40024ffd13a9f48040de759f5d7` and defaults to `?world=core`.
2. Core World owns world identity, a 48 m uniform grid, a seven-by-seven active set, focus, provider ordering, cell lifecycle, diagnostics, and portable snapshots.
3. MyCozyIsland owns seven ordered terrain/classification/population/presentation providers and their heavy runtime stores.
4. The compatibility bridge creates a legacy renderer snapshot from the active provider records.
5. The route calls that bridge once during startup and constructs one whole-island renderer.
6. Later Core World focus commits do not commit updated presentation descriptors or resource deltas to the renderer.
7. Cell-cache and disposal utilities exist and have isolated Node fixtures, but the production host does not wire the cell-aware renderer controller.
8. `pagehide` disposes the Core World domain only. It does not clear the animation loop, remove listeners, cancel loader timers, dispose the Three/WebGPU resource graph, or retire the global host.
