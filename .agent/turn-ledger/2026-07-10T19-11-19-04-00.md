## Findings

1. `renderers.js` makes the layered wrapper the public world renderer.
2. The wrapper removes `grass-patch` rows only from the snapshot passed to the base renderer.
3. The original rows are projected into a separate instanced grass mesh.
4. The atlas texture is created inside the material factory and is not retained by a named owner.
5. The inner renderer object is not preserved by the outer wrapper after its group is attached.
6. The inner and outer renderers lack `getState()` and `dispose()`.
7. The base renderer also lacks lifecycle composition.
8. Host diagnostics cannot prove source reconciliation, resource creation, or disposal.
9. Existing Node tests do not execute the browser renderer path.
